Project: Book Worms

Book Worms Early Learning Center is a day care located in Old City, Philadelphia. They are a small company that was not only looking for a redesign to attract new clients but also for a site that would be more accessible to parents of current students. I built the site using WordPress to allow my client to easily make updates and to move the weekly emails she had been sending out to parents to a new, password protected page parents would now be able to reference at anytime. Before, the application was a PDF that was difficult to read and needed to be printed out. I created an online application that will make it much easier for Book Worms to organize and much easier for potential clients to fill out.

For the design, it was important for me to keep it playful, in the spirit of a day care, but also clean and easy for current parents as well as potential clients to find what they are looking for respectively.

Project: Monolith Skate

Monolith Skate is a mobile skateboard shop which will sell skateboard apparel and gear from a restored 1950s ice cream truck. Although the site is static html, there are many features that will still allow the client to make updates easily. The events page has an RSS feed that links to a Blogger account, allowing events to be posted easily from their Google account. I also incorporated Google latitude along with their Twitter account so they can easily tell visitors where the truck is. Another function I added to the site was an Instagram feed that allows photos from the Monolith feed to display when a specific hashtag is used.

The Monolith site is responsive which was important to my client since a lot of customers would be viewing the site, possibly at an event and most likely on a mobile device. I incorporated touch capabilities to easily swipe through the product page if viewed on a touch screen device.

Project: She Tech Philly

She Tech Philly was a project that came from a hackathon in March 2013. This was not only my first hackathon but also my first time collaborating with so many others on a website. The site was developed by myself and six other ladies in the hopes of creating a site that would be a "one stop shop" for women in tech in the Philadelphia area. Our immediate goals were to create a site that gathered all events related to women and tech into one place. We also blog about events and our experiences as women in tech. We are continuing to work on the site and have big aspirations for where it will go in the future.

Working on this site with such a large group has been such a great learning experience. The members of She Tech Philly come from all different backgrounds and skill levels so it has been a fun experience figuring out where each one of us fits within the project. Since it is a side project and more of a labor of love we have also had to figure out the most effective ways of communicating with one another and ways we could find time to help the project grow.
